## Guidelines for Analysis

### Identifying the Operational Bottleneck
Look for these common patterns:
- **Manual Communication Overload**: Relying on FB comments/DMs, Gmail, phone calls
- **No Self-Service**: Customers can't book, price, or schedule without back-and-forth
- **Location Confusion**: Multiple locations with unclear service areas or offerings
- **Trust/Credibility Gap**: Using emoji-heavy marketplace ads instead of professional presence
- **Lost Leads**: High engagement but no systematic way to capture and convert
- **One-Off Revenue**: No recurring/subscription model despite repeat customers

### Choosing the Right Aesthetic
Match the visual style to the industry:
- **Emergency/Urgent Services** (Roofing, Septic, Plumbing): Storm Warning, Safety Yellow/Black, Municipal Utility
- **Premium/Luxury** (Moving, Cleaning, Private Services): Clean minimalism, Navy/Sky Blue, Soft pastels
- **Family/Education** (Childcare, Tutoring, Camps): Heritage Modern, Warm earth tones, High-production photography
- **Industrial/Logistics** (Junk Removal, Landscaping, Construction): Concrete Gray, Electric Blue, Stencil typography
- **Local Authority** (Handyman, Regional Services): Dark Mode, Electric Orange, Brutalist typography

### Creating "Overly Amazing" Features
These should feel like **enterprise-grade technology** applied to a small business:
- **Real-time data displays**: Live counters, capacity meters, availability trackers
- **AI/Automation framing**: "Satellite analysis", "Volumetric scanning", "Priority damage codes"
- **Operational transparency**: Live dispatch maps, field unit tracking, storm/weather HUDs
- **Urgency mechanisms**: Limited slots, countdown timers, fleet capacity warnings
- **Tech-forward UX**: Member portals, direct-to-dash uplinks, webhook routing language

## Important Notes
- **Be specific** about colors, typography, and layout structure
- **Avoid underscores in UI text** unless it's part of the technical/brutalist aesthetic
- **Make each demo unique** - don't repeat the same features across different businesses
- **Focus on the transformation** - what makes this business feel 10x more professional than their current presence
- **Create urgency** - limited slots, live availability, real-time updates
- **Frame as infrastructure** - not just a website, but a "hub", "center", "portal", "system"
